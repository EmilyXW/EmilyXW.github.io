Hobbies

Art

I love art because it's the perfect outlet for endless creativity and exploration. I've learned a lot from creating, whether it be drawing techniques or lessons on perseverance. Making art can be frustrating at times, but it's not something I'll ever give up.

I'm most comfortable with pencil portraits, though over the past few years I've challenged myself to explore new mediums and subjects, as well as enter art competitions. One of my drawings has received a High Merit award in Celebrating Art's art anthology.

Dance

I also love dance. Though I do get nervous before competing on stage, I always enjoy my time practicing and performing. Dance has taught me to have more confidence in myself, as well as that consistent effort and thorough preparation go a long way. I grew up constantly reading a poster in my dance studio that said, “Never settle for less than your best”. Sadly, the poster's been taken down, but I still apply its motto to everything I do. Overall, I’d say that dance has played a fundamental role in my growth, and I look forward to continuing my journey to improve. I danced at Greendale Dance Academy; here's their website!

I am still dancing at Cornell, though now I’m trying new styles like Chinese traditional and lion dance. I thought I’d stop dancing in college, but it seems I just couldn’t resist the lure of challenging myself with new styles and performing once more under warm stage lights.

Clubs

I’m part of Illuminations, Lion Dance, Guiding Eyes for the Blind, and Guild of Visual Arts at Cornell. I love creating, biomedical engineering topics, and working on things hands-on, so I’m hoping to get on a project team this year as well. I had so much fun and learned so much making an assistive technology project in STEM II at MAMS that I can’t wait to work on more projects at Cornell. By joining a project team, I hope to learn even more about challenges in healthcare and develop skills in engineering to tackle them head-on!

I participated in many of Mass Academy's clubs, like Birdwatching, the school newspaper, Cooking Club, and Computer-Aided Design (CAD). While I was at my sending school, Westborough High School, I was a member in clubs including Science Olympiad, Art Club, and Math Team.

Community Service

I volunteered at the Aging Well Adult Day Health Center and the Friends Bookstore at the Worcester Public Library. Both places are full of kind, thoughtful people, so I always enjoy my time volunteering.

Aging Well is a center that provides elders in the community with enrichment and care. There are many fun activities and welcoming staff there, so it's a wonderful place to be! I've helped out with many things during my time at Aging Well, from serving meals to leading games and translating questions for the nurses.

The Friends Bookstore is a bookstore that is completely volunteer-run, and all profits go towards supporting the Worcester Public Library and its community. My favorite job I do there is sorting through and cleaning up books, though I also work the cash register, assist customers, and help organize the store. The bookstore could always use more customers and volunteers!

Animals

Since I have no siblings, I begged my parents for a dog or cat growing up. Though they finally relented after over a decade, the damage from years without a furry companion had been done: I’d developed an ever-growing obsession with my friends’ pets and other animals. Thus, I ended up shadowing at a veterinary clinic. At Cornell, I'm part of Guiding Eyes for the Blind, a club that raises seeing eye dogs. There couldn’t be a more perfect club for me. I get to play with puppies and help improve the lives of people in need of service dogs!
